### About Page Text
The Red Poppy Art House is a room on a corner in the Mission District of San Francisco, nestled between a myriad of communities. It is a place for slowness and the nuanced intermingling of ideas and activities generally termed "art," presenting a vigorous performance program spanning multiple disciplines and embracing both traditional and contemporary forms. The organization also hosts artist residencies, a socially engaged professional development track, and assists in curating space for the neighborhood-wide bimonthly MAPP happening (Mission Arts & Performance Project).

### Mission & Vision (/about/mission-vision/)
**Mission:** The Poppy is an artist-driven organization that seeks to empower and transform society by addressing current social issues that impact our community and society at large through creative processes. More specifically, the intent of Red Poppy Art House is to forge a bridge between high caliber artistic work and community life through visibility and inclusiveness.

**Vision/Founding Purpose:** Founded in 2003 to serve as an intercultural and multidisciplinary "space of encounter" - a hub where multiple social-cultural groups could interconnect to experience one another and therefore potentiate one another's endeavors while weaving a more solid and tolerant social fabric.

Operating from a neighborhood storefront in San Francisco's Mission District, we demonstrate the unique, powerful, and irreplaceable capacity of intimate community spaces through hosting over 150 diverse performances, exhibitions, workshops, and artist residencies annually.

The organization enjoys a sturdy connection to its working-class Latino community via the longstanding Mission Arts and Performance Project series, a popular weekly family art program, a professional development workshop for artists and more.

### Art House Story (/about/art-house-story/)
- **February 2003**: Alexander Allende and Todd Brown signed a lease on a big empty room, originally named "Porfilio Is"
- Began as a working artist studio that offered classes in painting and Argentine tango, a weekly jazz night, and curated exhibitions
- **End of 2003**: A small group of artists staged the first "Mission Arts Party" (later renamed Mission Arts & Performance Project: MAPP), a bimonthly community arts festival
- **2004**: Todd joined forces with Mark Eisner, a Visiting Scholar in Latin American Studies at Stanford, who had founded a nonprofit arts organization called "Red Poppy" (named for the flower in Pablo Neruda's poetry). Mark was creating a documentary on Neruda and had finished "The Essential Neruda" (City Lights). The space was renamed "Red Poppy Art House."
- **2006**: Meklit Hadero joined Todd. Through a two-year dual-directorship model, they formalized the Poppy as a fiscally sponsored organization and sought out the first relationships with funders
- **2007**: Gained fiscal sponsorship through Intersection for the Arts, joining their Arts Incubator program
- The Art House attracted ever-increasing participation from artists, cultural thinkers, volunteers, and community members, evolving into the intercultural hub it is today
- Currently working on transition to a formalized 501(c)(3) non-profit organization

## PAGE 4: VOLUNTEER (/participate/volunteer/)

### Volunteer Page Content
The Red Poppy Art House is primarily an artist and volunteer-run organization, and volunteers play an integral role in their everyday operations, from assisting with event productions to stocking up on concessions.

The Art House produces 150 events annually and is always looking for people to come out once or twice a month to assist.

**Benefits:** For every shift worked, each volunteer receives one free pass to another Poppy event.

**Volunteer Shifts Include:**
- Helping to set up the performance space
- Engaging with guests and community members
- Occupying one of two roles:
  1. Handling admissions at the door (welcoming guests as they arrive)
  2. Concessions at the counter

**How It Works:**
- Each night requires two volunteers (sign up with a friend!)
- Volunteers come out once or twice a month

**Contact:** volunteer@redpoppyarthouse.org

## PAGE 5: GIVE (/participate/give/)

### Donation Options

**Online Donations:**
- Donations of any amount provide pivotal support
- Made through fiscal sponsor Intersection for the Arts via the Flipcause platform
- You may bring cash, check, debit or credit card, or donate online

**Patron Membership:**
- Become an official Red Poppy Art House Patron by joining their membership base
- Contributions as low as $5-10/month
- **Patron Benefits:**
  - Free entry for you and one guest to one RPAH performance of your choice per month (RSVP required 1 week in advance)
  - Private meet and greet for you and up to 3 guests with a featured artist from the community once per year, hosted over dinner at the RPAH
  - Patrons make a significant investment in the Red Poppy's upcoming organizational goals including the transition to a formalized 501(c)(3)

**Patreon (patreon.com/RedPoppy):**
- Various tiers supporting different aspects:
  - Art materials for Family Art program (free arts activities for neighborhood youth)
  - Ongoing expenses (paint for exhibitions, replacement lighting, sound equipment)
  - Free and accessible space for Artists-in-Residence
- **Patreon Rewards:**
  - Invitation to annual RPAH Patreon Gratitude Dinner
  - Private viewings with complimentary aperitif of any RPAH exhibition (for patron + 3 guests)
  - Priority entry and reserved seats for RPAH events
  - Free tickets and private exhibition viewings

**Wish List Donations:**
- One of the most accessible ways to help support the Red Poppy
- Contact info@redpoppyarthouse.org for current wish list items

**Corporate Match (Benevity):**
- If your employer has a corporate matching program, search on Benevity for Intersection for the Arts and select Red Poppy as the recipient to have your donation doubled

**Ticket Philosophy:**
- Door admissions from shows are always split with performing artists
- Majority goes directly to performers, small percentage supports RPAH operating costs
- Guests are invited to consider their contribution as a direct "audience grant" supporting the arts economy of San Francisco

## PROGRAMS (/programs/)

### 1. Performance Series (/programs/performance-series/)
- Diverse and international program of artists working in both traditional and contemporary performing arts
- Music, poetry, dance, literary events, lectures
- Sliding scale admission typically $15-$25
- Door admissions split with performing artists (majority to performers)

### 2. Exhibitions (/programs/exhibitions/)
- Does not usually host formal exhibitions
- Presents works throughout the year via informal rotations of works for sale
- Socially engaged large-scale installation pieces
- Featured artists include: Paz de la Calzada, Alberto Toscano, Fernando Diaz, Yolanda Lopez, Adriana Camarena

### 3. Artist Residencies (/programs/artist-residencies/)
- Free and accessible space for Artists-in-Residence to rehearse, develop, and explore
- **Crossover Collaborative Residencies** (`/programs/artist-residencies/crossover-residencies/`)
  - Supported by Kenneth Rainin Foundation and San Francisco Arts Commission
  - Interdisciplinary program pairing 6 choreographer/dancers with 6 composer/musicians
  - Four-stage development:
    1. Preliminary artist gatherings and orientation workshops
    2. Early-stage "raw" presentations of works in progress
    3. Middle-stage free community-engaged presentation
    4. Final performance/presentation
  - Unique orientation process designed to support culturally insightful/aware collaborative processes
- **A Feast of Communities** (`/programs/artist-residencies/a-feast-of-communities/`)
  - Limited information available online

### 4. MAPP - Mission Arts & Performance Project (/programs/the-mapp-program/)
- Incubated in 2003 by the Red Poppy Art House
- Homegrown bi-monthly, multidisciplinary, intercultural happening in the Mission District
- Started as the "Mission Arts Party" at the end of 2003
- Continues to this day as a neighborhood-wide arts event

### 5. Family Art (/programs/family-art/)
- Also called "Arte entre Familia"
- Free quality art programming for families in the Mission district for over 10 years
- Bilingual Spanish/English program
- Held Saturdays (schedule varies - some sources say every Saturday, others say one Saturday per month from 12:00-1:30pm)
- Takes place outdoors on the corner of Folsom Street and 23rd Street
- Invites all families with children 0-5, youth, and adults
- Art and community facilitator creates safe and creative space for open-ended art projects
- Supported by Sam Mazza Foundation and Art4Moore

### 6. Mission Stoop Fest (/programs/mission-stoop-fest/)
- Formerly called "PORCH" (renamed to avoid confusion with another Porchfest)
- Civil society arts experiment on front stoops of local residences
- Traditional and contemporary artists perform; local residents share stories and histories
- The front stoop represents a literal threshold between public and private spheres
- Provides program and map of multiple neighborhood stoops
- Occurs during the day on same bi-monthly dates as MAPP

### 7. SF Youth Theatre (/programs/san-francisco-youth-theatre/)
- San Francisco Youth Theatre (SFYT)
- Cutting-edge theater for children and youth ages 3-24
- Offers classes and performance ensembles
- Produces adult-performed productions for young audiences
- School residencies
- Repertoire of original productions

### 8. FRESH Festival (/programs/fresh-festival/)
- San Francisco's annual festival of experimental dance, music, and performance
- Starts the new year with an extravaganza of embodied art, action, and interaction
- FRESH Exchanges offer interactive, inclusive, insightful gatherings at RPAH:
  - **RIPE** (raw + intimate performance experiments) - two artists share new material with moderated feedback
  - **POTLUCK/Social Practice** - collaborative coexistence and conversations with warm food
  - **Alternative Conservatory** - lecture/performance, panel discussion, and workshop
- Affordable social events to cultivate, cross-pollinate, and invigorate dance, music, and performance communities

### 9. Development Track (Professional Development)
- PRESENCE - the Red Poppy Art House Development Cohort
- Socially engaged professional development track
- Training in community arts presentation through workshops and internships
- Visiting artists and cultural workers lead sessions
- Fertile, informal space for gathering, discussion, and artistic cross-pollination

## RENTALS (/rentals/)

### Space Details
- 650-square-foot homey space
- Seats up to 49 people
- Equipped with:
  - Small kitchen
  - Quality sound equipment for live performance
  - French-made Pleyel upright piano
  - Natural light
  - Cozy nooks

### Rates
- Rates vary based on event type and day of week
- Starting from approximately $150
- Reception fee approximately $1,200 (includes 8 hours including setup and cleanup)
- Reduced rates available for non-profit organizations

### Policies
- Must close any rental by 10:00PM (out of respect for neighbors above)
- Cancellation 30+ days in advance: full refund
- Cancellation 30-7 days in advance: 50% refund
- Cancellations within 7 days: non-refundable
